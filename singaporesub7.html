Building a social life post-secondary school


The TL;DR: The Straits Times answers five questions new junior college, polytechnic and Institute of Technical
Education (ITE) students might have about friendships after graduating from secondary school.

1. Now that there are no longer fixed classes, is it still important to form meaningful connections? How should
one do so?

Mr Dominic Chan, 19, from ITE College Central, admitted that making friends in secondary school was “much easier”.

Upon starting at ITE, the Higher Nitec accounting student did not plan on making new friends. But during his
first year, Mr Chan realised the importance of taking the initiative to socialise with his peers.

For a business ethics module, he was grouped with others who were unable to find a group. “We were
allowed to choose (our teammates) but, unfortunately, at that time I was not familiar with anyone and ended
up with strangers,” he said.

As his teammates did not share the same motivation or work ethic that he had, he had to take on a significant
amount of responsibility. Despite his best efforts, the outcome was not as good as he had hoped.
