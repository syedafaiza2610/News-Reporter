Angela Merkel’s World of Yesterday




Although just three years have passed since Merkel left office, the world has changed so dramatically that her chancellorship already feels like it belongs to a different era.

Angela Merkel, often referred to as the world’s most powerful woman, served as Chancellor of Germany from 2005 to 2021. Her tenure, spanning four terms, left an indelible mark on Germany, Europe, and global politics. Reflecting on her leadership is to examine a world shaped by pragmatism, resilience, and a commitment to consensus.

Merkel’s rise to power was itself a symbol of change. A physicist from former East Germany, she broke barriers as the first woman to lead Germany, bringing a scientific, methodical approach to politics. Her leadership style was characterized by cautious decision-making, often prioritizing stability over bold, sweeping changes. This approach earned her respect as a steady hand during turbulent times, though critics sometimes labeled her policies as too slow-moving.

During her years in office, Merkel navigated Germany and the European Union through numerous crises. The 2008 global financial collapse tested her economic leadership, as Germany emerged as a pillar of strength in the Eurozone. Her insistence on fiscal discipline and financial reforms helped stabilize the region, but it also drew criticism from nations struggling under austerity measures.

During her years in office, Merkel navigated Germany and the European Union through numerous crises. The 2008 global financial collapse tested her economic leadership, as Germany emerged as a pillar of strength in the Eurozone. Her insistence on fiscal discipline and financial reforms helped stabilize the region, but it also drew criticism from nations struggling under austerity measures. The refugee crisis of 2015 became a defining moment of her career. Merkel’s decision to open Germany’s borders to over a million refugees, many fleeing conflict in Syria, demonstrated her commitment to humanitarian values. Her statement, “Wir schaffen das” (“We can do it”), resonated with many but also polarized public opinion. The move reshaped Germany’s social fabric and political landscape, fueling the rise of far-right movements but also inspiring a new era of multiculturalism and integration.

Merkel’s global influence extended far beyond Germany’s borders. She was a key figure in the European Union, often mediating disputes and driving cohesion within the bloc. Her leadership during Brexit negotiations, her handling of Russian aggression under Vladimir Putin, and her steady stance on climate change positioned her as a cornerstone of international diplomacy. In a world increasingly dominated by populist leaders, Merkel’s measured, understated approach offered a contrast that emphasized collaboration over confrontation.
