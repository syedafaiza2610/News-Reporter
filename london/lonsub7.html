Met investigates how bus driven after pensioner dies

The Metropolitan Police are investigating an incident involving a bus that was reportedly driven away after a pensioner died on board. The tragic event has raised serious questions about the circumstances surrounding the death and the subsequent actions of the bus operator. Witnesses reported that the elderly passenger appeared to be in distress before collapsing, and despite efforts by fellow passengers to assist, the individual was pronounced dead at the scene. The investigation is focusing on why the bus continued its route after the fatality, prompting concerns about potential breaches of protocol. Authorities are working to establish a timeline of events and determine whether proper procedures were followed in handling the situation. This includes assessing the response of the driver, the bus company, and any emergency services involved. Public transport operators are typically required to stop service and cooperate fully in cases of medical emergencies or fatalities, leaving many to question why this protocol may not have been adhered to.
